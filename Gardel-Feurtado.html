The Analyst in Paramaribo

Gardel Feurtado's Assignment to Suriname During the Reagan Era

An Expert in Instability

The assignment of Gardel Feurtado to Paramaribo, Suriname, in March 1982 was no coincidence. It represented a deliberate move by the Reagan administration, placing an analyst with specific, relevant expertise into a geopolitical hotspot at the precise moment of its eruption. This infographic visualizes the career path and critical timing that highlight the integration of U.S. intelligence and diplomacy.

Feurtado's Career Trajectory

1. The Academic Foundation

Feurtado's early work established him as a specialist in the very conditions he would later be sent to analyze: regime change and political realignment.

1972 PUBLICATION Article on Chinese revolutionary committees during the Cultural Revolution.
1979 DISSERTATION “Political Stability, Instability and Change”

2. From Analyst to Field Officer: A Timeline

His transition from academia to the State Department was rapid, moving from intelligence analysis to the Foreign Service, and then specializing in the Caribbean.

1980: Intelligence Analyst

Listed in the Bureau of Intelligence and Research (INR), Office of Global Issues (INR/GI).

Jan 1981: Junior Officer Corps

Newly appointed, marking his formal entry into the service.

Mar 1981: Foreign Service Officer

Formally confirmed as a Foreign Service Officer and consular officer.

Early 1982: Caribbean Desk

In the Office of Caribbean Affairs, covering Dutch-language territories (Leeward & Netherlands Antilles).

March 1982: Assignment Paramaribo

Assigned to Dutch language training, en route to the U.S. Embassy in Paramaribo, Suriname.

The Suriname Context: March 1982

The timing of Feurtado's assignment was not routine. It coincided exactly with a violent coup attempt against the Bouterse regime, a period of maximum instability that demanded expert analysis on the ground.

U.S. Personnel Action

MARCH 1982

State Department announces assignment of Gardel Feurtado—an INR analyst specializing in instability—to Paramaribo.

Event in Suriname

MARCH 1982

The Rambocus/Hawker coup attempt occurs, severely challenging Dési Bouterse's regime and creating extreme political volatility.
